Labor Market Comparisons

Prompt:

Premium Paper Products (PPP) is the only employer in the small town of Gandor. PPP is a monopsonist in the labor market and a perfect competitor in the paper market. When operating in the short run, labor is its only variable resource. The graph above demonstrates the labor market for PPP.

Questions:

1. Looking at the graph, what kind of monopsonist is the firm? How can you tell?

The firm is a non-discriminating monopsony, as the MCL curve has a different slope than the supply of labor and is greater than the supply of labor curve.

2. Using the information in the graph, what is the profit maximizing quantity of labor for PPP?

Profit maximization occurs where MCL = MRP, which occurs at 50 laborers with wages of $15. Competitive equilibrium, however, occurs at 100 laborers and a wage of $20.

3. Identify the wage rate PPP pays to hire the profit maximizing quantity of labor. Are all employees paid that rate or just the last employee hired?

$15. In a non-discriminating monopsony, all laborers are paid the same wage as the last one hired.

4. What wage would a competitive labor market dictate PPP pay its employees? How many laborers would be employed?

Competitive equilibrium occurs where MRP = S, or $20. 100 laborers would be employed here.

5. If the government enacts a minimum wage of $17.00, what will be the impact on the quantity of labor hired by PPP?

A minimum wage of $17.00 exceeds the profit maximizing point for PPP. Unable to employ 50 laborers at a wage of $15.00, PPP will likely increase labor to at least 75 or greater (such as to 100 and competitive equilibrium) where wages will meet or exceed minimum wage. Minimum wage does not have to cause unemployment in the case of a monopsony.
